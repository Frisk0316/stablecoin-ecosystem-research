# Stablecoin Ecosystem Research Report v0.1

## Status

This is a first-version structured research report. It is designed to consolidate the initial findings from the source registry, issuer matrix, claim table, central-bank materials, law/regulation sources, and payment-settlement documents.

## Core thesis

Stablecoins are best understood as a reconfiguration of the dollar financial system rather than an external replacement for it. Fiat-backed stablecoins package bank deposits, U.S. Treasury bills, repo/reverse repo, money market funds, custodians, regulated issuers, compliance controls, and blockchain transferability into one instrument.

## 1. Issuer landscape

The issuer matrix currently covers USDC, USDT, PYUSD, USDP, USDG, RLUSD, FDUSD, GUSD, DAI/USDS, USDe, and USDPT/UDSPT. These assets should be separated into at least three types:

- Fiat-backed payment stablecoins: USDC, USDT, PYUSD, USDP, USDG, RLUSD, FDUSD, GUSD.
- Crypto/RWA-collateralized protocol stablecoins: DAI / USDS.
- Synthetic-dollar instruments: USDe.

USDPT/UDSPT remains an open product-level research topic because current documentation is not sufficient to define its full reserve, redemption, or settlement design.

## 2. Reserves and dollar money markets

Issuer reserve documents and central-bank research indicate that stablecoins are tied to short-term safe assets. BIS and ECB sources support the view that stablecoin growth can affect short-term Treasury markets and the broader safe-asset channel.

## 3. Regulation

U.S. sources include GENIUS Act, CLARITY Act, OCC NPRM, Treasury state-level principles, and AML/CFT materials. EU sources include EBA/MiCA implementation materials. UK sources include BoE/FPC materials. NYDFS remains high-priority because RLUSD and GUSD reserve reports rely on NYDFS stablecoin criteria.

## 4. Central-bank and international institution views

BIS provides the main conceptual lens: singleness, elasticity and integrity. ECB sources emphasize monetary sovereignty, safe-asset channels and deposit substitution. FSB sources document the global regulatory baseline and implementation gaps. Taiwan CBC sources provide a Taiwan-specific angle on NTD stablecoins, USD stablecoins, monetary statistics, credit creation and FX monitoring.

## 5. Payment and settlement

The payment-settlement module should not treat SWIFT as a funds-settlement system. CPMI sources establish the correspondent banking baseline and the cost/speed/transparency problems of cross-border retail payments. Western Union, Anchorage, Fedwire and World Bank sources support the USDPT/UDSPT research path.

## 6. Open research gaps

- NYDFS stablecoin guidance original source.
- IMF stablecoin papers.
- Federal Reserve stablecoin papers and speeches.
- Visa, DeFiLlama, Cambridge and Artemis data exports.
- USDPT/UDSPT product page, reserve report and terms.
- Failure-case sources for Terra UST, Iron Finance, USDC SVB depeg and USDT reserve controversy.

## 7. Next step

The next phase should be page-level extraction. Each issuer should receive a verified row for issuer entity, regulator, reserve composition, redemption rights, eligible direct redeemers, report/attestation type, yield or reward treatment, and freeze/blacklist/suspension powers.
