# Executive Summary v0.1

This first delivery establishes a structured evidence base for researching stablecoins as an extension of the dollar financial system. The strongest current evidence supports four preliminary findings:

1. Major fiat-backed stablecoins are connected to the traditional dollar system through reserve assets such as bank deposits, Treasury bills, repo-like instruments, and money market funds.
2. Stablecoins differ materially by design: fiat-backed payment stablecoins, crypto/RWA-backed protocol stablecoins, and synthetic-dollar instruments should not be treated as the same risk class.
3. Central banks and international institutions are concerned with singleness, elasticity, integrity, financial stability, bank disintermediation, monetary sovereignty, and safe-asset market effects.
4. USDPT/UDSPT should be analysed through payment-system architecture: messaging, correspondent banking, settlement assets, Western Union’s agent network, and stablecoin reserve/redemption mechanics.

The project is not yet ready for a final report because NYDFS, IMF, Federal Reserve, on-chain dashboard data, and USDPT product-level documents still require final external fetch or manual download.
